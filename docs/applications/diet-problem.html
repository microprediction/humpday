🥗 Least-Cost Diet

Choose quantities of eight foods to meet five nutrient requirements at minimum cost (Stigler's classic diet problem). Each food has a price and a nutrient profile, and falling short of any requirement is penalised. Cheap foods rarely cover every nutrient alone, so the optimum mixes a few complementary ones. Score is food cost plus unmet-requirement penalty, to minimise. 8-D problem.
