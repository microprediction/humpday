💲 Revenue Pricing

A firm sets prices for eight customer segments. Each segment's demand falls linearly with price, q = a − b·p, and all segments draw on one limited capacity. Price too low and demand overruns capacity (penalised); too high and revenue collapses. The optimum is capacity-constrained segment pricing, raising prices on the segments that consume scarce capacity most. Score is negative revenue plus an over-capacity penalty. 8-D.
