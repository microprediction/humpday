What's happening

The course is a flat green with a vertical wall mid-fairway, a sand bunker just past it that slows the ball, and a hole at the far right end. The optimiser sets three numbers — angle, power, sidespin — and a 2-D rigid-body simulator does the rest.

Multiple strategies exist for this layout:

  • Putt under the wall — too short and you hit the wall; too hard and you blast past the hole.
  • Chip over the wall — high arc, moderate power; lands on the green between the wall and the bunker, rolls toward the hole.
  • Sidespin hook — curve around the wall; needs precise spin + angle combination.

The integer-rounded distance term gives only a weak signal between far-off shots; algorithms that explore broadly tend to find the chip-over-wall basin sooner. Once they've found it, local refinement methods (PRIMA_BOBYQA, NelderMead) close on the precise angle/power quickly.
