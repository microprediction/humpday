What's happening

A top-down 2-D curling sheet: hack on the left, house on the right. The house has four concentric rings (12-foot, 8-foot, 4-foot, button). Three red opponent stones are already in play — two guards in front of the house and one parked on the button. You throw a single yellow stone from the left and choose four release parameters: line, weight, rotation, sweep release.

The lateral curl of the stone is modelled as a force proportional to rotation × (1 / current speed) — so slower stones curl more, matching real-ice behaviour. Sweeping reduces friction so the stone stays alive longer; in this model that increases the integrated curl (more time on ice ≈ more lateral drift). The sweep release parameter sets how far down the sheet sweeping continues before full friction resumes — so high values give more curl, low values give a flatter line.

Score = 100 × (1 − distance_to_button / house_radius) if your stone ends up closer to the button than any opponent stone, else 0. Knocking the parked opponent out of the house is usually necessary to score, since it sits on the button by default.

Physics powered by Matter.js — rigid-body collisions, friction. Curl is modelled as an additional lateral force on the stone proportional to its angular velocity and inverse linear speed.
